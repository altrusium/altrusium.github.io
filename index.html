Altrusium

an element of altruism

Hi, I'm Don, and I created Altrusium as a way to make money for charities.

With a wife and a daughter both attending university and only one income (my fulltime job), I've had to reduce the amount I'm able to give to charity. So I thought I could take a few of the ideas I've been developing and turn them into revenue donation streams.

Why not just donate my time and energies directly to the nonprofits? I'm doing that too, but this approach has advantages. I'm able to work on the projects in my own time and have more granular control over the priorities and dependencies. Also, many of my ideas are very start-upy and I'd like to gain the experience of taking projects to market with ultra-small teams so I can continue to do so with future ideas.

As long as I have a traditional fulltime job, I will NOT use any Altrusium income for myself. 100% of money raised from Altrusium projects will be donated to nonprofits. My normal income will pay for all my bills and expenses. The software and hosting costs? Fortunately Microsoft has accepted me for the BizSpark program, which gives me access to heaps of free software and a generous amount of cloud hosting. Anything else will be open source or donationware, which I happily support as a normal expense.

The list is always changing, but this is the current list of charities I am supporting:

  • Charity Water
  • Tennis Auckland
  • Survival International
  • Children International
  • The Planetary Society
  • Sustainable Coastlines
  • And more ...

I'll post a listing of products and projects as they become ready. Promise :)
